01
Stack the Germany game
Germany vs Curaçao (Jun 14) is the highest projected total of Week 1. 4+ goals expected. Load 3-4 German attackers. Yes, it feels obvious — but it's the right play. Don't be contrarian just to be different.
02
Wait on your captain decision
If you're in a daily format, wait until 2 hours before the last game of the day to set your captain. You'll have injury news and early match feedback to work with. Musiala (Jun 14), Messi (Jun 16), and Yamal (Jun 15) are all in play.
03
Underown Haaland this week
Norway's group reputation scares people off Haaland. But Iraq are defensively porous — 1.9 goals conceded per 90 in qualifying. At $7.5M he's priced 15% below his ceiling. Most lineups will skip him. Exploit the low ownership.
04
Don't stack the Haiti match
Haiti vs Scotland is projected to be the lowest-scoring game of the week. Under 2.5 is the consensus line. Two defensive teams with no genuine attacking star power. Avoid both squads unless you have a specific tactical read.
05
Midfielder value beats forwards this week
In matches where goals are expected (Germany, Spain, Belgium, Argentina), the midfielders who set up goals score nearly as many points as the forwards — at lower price points. Bruno Fernandes, De Bruyne, and Pedri all offer goal+assist upside at lower cost than a second forward.
06
Check GK + defender bonus
Germany vs Curaçao, Spain vs Cape Verde, and France vs Senegal all project for clean sheets. If your format gives bonus points for clean sheets, GK Neuer (GER), Unai Simón (ESP), or Maignan (FRA) are high-floor plays in cash games.
