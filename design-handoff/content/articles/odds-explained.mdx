## Moneylines Tell You the Market's Winner

A moneyline is the price on a team to win, or on the draw. A favorite at -145 implies roughly 59% before sportsbook margin. A dog at +300 implies roughly 25%.

That implied number is the baseline. PitchIQ looks for places where the model disagrees with the market.

## Totals Are About Game Shape

Totals price the number of goals. Over 2.5 needs at least three goals. Under 2.5 wins at zero, one, or two.

The best totals reads usually come from tempo, finishing quality, and game state. A heavy favorite that scores early can break an under fast.

## Spreads Are Margin Bets

Soccer spreads are often listed as handicaps. France -1.5 means France must win by two or more. Morocco +1.5 means Morocco covers with a win, draw, or one-goal loss.

The handicap can be better than the moneyline when the favorite's price is too expensive.

## Implied Probability Is the Filter

Convert the line before you trust the take. If -145 implies 59% and your read gives the team 66%, there may be value. If your read gives them 55%, skip it.
